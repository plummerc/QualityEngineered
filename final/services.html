The different types of testing can be compared to making or consuming chocolate chip cookies. Depending on the tester's level of technical knowledge and depending on the testing goal, there are hundreds of variations of testing.

You could be the baker (or, with automation, the operations manager), checking the quality of your ingredients or checking the final result by having one. You could be the consumer, with no knowledge of how the cookies were made, what ingredients were used, all you want to know is do they taste good! Or you could be an experienced food critic, who knows what ingredients are required and how to prepare and achieve the final result, and you're eating cookies to analyze more than just whether they taste good. Or you could be anywhere in between.

All three individuals care about quality, but are able to analyze quality from different levels of technical knowledge and for various end goals. This explains the different levels of testing in a nutshell: white box, gray box, and black box. Visit each one's specific page to learn more about the different types of goals and skills required to perform each.
