Black Box Testing

Continuing with the analogy of baking and consuming chocolate chip cookies, Black Box Testing is most typically akin to the consumer's process for reviewing the end product.
The consumer likely has no knowledge of how the cookie was made, what ingredients were used or where they were sourced from. They are purely concerned with a satisfactory end product, that will not give them salmonella poisoning. No level of technical knowledge is required for black box testing. A cookie that was made with salt, instead of flour, would fail the consumer's test immediately. White box testing can pass with flying colors and black box testing could still fail.
The focus on the consumer requirements required by black box testing makes it the most common form, but it is often overlooked that it can be performed throughout any phase of the software development life cycle.
