White Box Testing

Continuing with the analogy of baking and consuming chocolate chip cookies, White Box Testing is most typically akin to the baker's ingredient source or the combinations of ingredients in order to correctly prepare the dough.
From the perspective of an ingredient source, let's say an egg supplier. Eggs may be used for millions of recipes, the supplier doesn't always know where their eggs will end up or how they will be prepared, but the supplier's concern is to provide the utmost quality in each egg, or each unit, as best as possible. So they will have a Quality Management System in place to ensure that their product, and their reputation, is and will remain superior to competitors.
Alternatively, from the perspective of the baker, they know that all the ingredients required and the order in which the ingredients must be combined. There is a process of inputs and outputs which, when followed correctly, will guarantee the perfect dough, each and every time.
The bakers and the suppliers are all performing white box testing when they examine the individual inputs and outputs that will contribute to or provides the foundation of a final product. The supplier doesn't necessarily care about what their eggs are used for, they care that they can be used and that they can be relied upon. This level of granularity is necessary for a code base as well. Code bases are comprised of units of functionality, that when syntactically and logically correct, will lead to a better product overall.
The more comprehensive your white box testing can be, the less rework, time, and money required down the line.
